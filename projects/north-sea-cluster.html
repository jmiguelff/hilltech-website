Project Overview

Redefining Offshore Reliability

The North Sea Cluster is one of Europe's largest offshore wind developments, spanning three interconnected sub-parks across the Danish and German sectors of the North Sea. When the operating consortium approached Hill Tech in 2022, the 96-turbine fleet was suffering from ageing power converter hardware, fragmented SCADA systems, and an unplanned downtime rate that significantly eroded annual energy production.

Hill Tech was contracted to design, supply, and commission full-power converter upgrades across the entire fleet, and to integrate all sub-park SCADA streams into a single unified monitoring platform. The programme was executed in three phases over 18 months ‚Äî with zero curtailment of grid obligations during the transition.

By the end of the first full operational year post-upgrade, unplanned downtime had fallen by 38%, mean time to repair dropped by 52%, and the fleet's average capacity factor improved by 3.1 percentage points ‚Äî translating to an additional 14,900 MWh of clean generation annually.

Scope of Work

What Hill Tech Delivered

An end-to-end technology programme spanning hardware, software, and long-term service support.

Full-Power Converter Upgrades

Designed and supplied 96 bespoke full-power converter assemblies rated for the harsh North Sea environment. Each unit features active thermal management, fully marinised enclosures rated to IP56, and modular power stacks enabling on-tower board replacement.

  • 5 MW / 690 V full-power topology
  • IGBT power stacks with redundant gate drives
  • Active liquid cooling with anti-corrosion loop
  • IEC 61400-21 grid-compliance testing

Unified SCADA Platform

Replaced three legacy SCADA systems with a single cloud-native platform providing real-time visibility, alarm management, and performance reporting across all 96 turbines and both offshore substations from a single pane of glass.

  • 10 ms data sampling across all turbines
  • IEC 61850 & OPC-UA data ingestion
  • Role-based access and audit logging
  • API integration with client ERP & trading systems

Condition Monitoring & Predictive Maintenance

Deployed accelerometer arrays and acoustic emission sensors on drivetrains and main bearings. Machine-learning models trained on fleet data provide 14-day advance warning of incipient faults, enabling condition-based maintenance scheduling.

  • Drivetrain vibration & acoustic monitoring
  • ML fault-detection with 94% precision
  • Automated work-order creation in client CMMS
  • Remote diagnostics centre (24 / 7)

Cybersecurity Hardening

Conducted a full IEC 62443 gap analysis and implemented OT network segmentation, encrypted SCADA communications, and continuous SOC monitoring to meet the NIS2 Directive requirements that came into force during the programme.

  • IEC 62443 SL-2 compliance achieved
  • Unidirectional security gateways installed
  • Encrypted fibre backbone (AES-256)
  • 24 / 7 SOC with OT-specific SIEM
